How to choose a racket
Match your racket to your skill level. Beginners benefit from lighter frames with larger head sizes for a bigger sweet spot. As you progress, move toward heavier, more precise frames that reward good technique.
Beginner
Light · Head-heavy · 105+ sq in
Intermediate
Mid · Even · 100–105 sq in
Professional
Heavy · Head-light · 95–100 sq in
Grip size guide
To find your grip size, place a ruler along your palm. Measure from the ring finger crease to the tip of the ring finger. Standard grips range from L1 (4") to L5 (4½").
Tip: When in doubt, choose a smaller grip — you can always add an overgrip for more thickness.
Weight & balance
Lighter rackets (under 275g) are easier to manoeuvre but offer less stability. Heavier frames (over 300g) deliver more power and dampening. Head-heavy frames add power; head-light frames improve control and reduce fatigue.
Tips for players
Demo before you buy · String at mid-tension to start · Restring every 3 months · Protect your frame from humidity
